Moving to Eatonton, GA? Your Complete New Resident Guide

March 3, 2026  ·  9 min read
← Back to Blog

So you're moving to Eatonton — or at least considering it. Welcome to Lake Country. Here's what you need to know.

The Big Picture

Eatonton is the county seat of Putnam County, located in Central Georgia about 90 miles east of Atlanta and 45 miles west of Augusta. Population in the city is around 6,500, with the county totaling approximately 22,000 residents. The median age is 48.9 years — reflecting a county that attracts retirees and second-home buyers alongside its longtime residents.

The Lakes

You can't talk about Putnam County without talking about the lakes. Lake Oconee (19,000 acres) and Lake Sinclair (15,300 acres) define the county's character and economy. Lake access — whether you buy on the water or just enjoy visiting — will be a central part of your life here.

Schools

The Putnam County Charter School System serves K-12 students across the county with a distinctive charter model emphasizing college and career readiness. Putnam County High School is home to the War Eagles. Private option Gatewood Schools also serves the community.

Healthcare

For primary care, Putnam County Family Health Center provides accessible care with sliding-scale fees. Lake Country Urgent Care handles walk-in needs with extended hours. The nearest major hospital is Navicent Health Baldwin in Milledgeville (about 20 miles south).

Local Favorites

Start at Yesterday Cafe for breakfast, Lake Country BBQ for lunch, and El Sombrero for dinner. Oconee Coffee Co. is your daily neighborhood coffee shop.

Community Life

The Putnam County Dairy Festival is the county's signature annual event. The Putnam County Library is an excellent community resource. Downtown Eatonton has an active merchant community and regularly hosts events and markets on the courthouse square.
