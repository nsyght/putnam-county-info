Putnam County Dairy Festival — What to Know

April 7, 2026  ·  4 min read
← Back to Blog

Every year, Eatonton comes alive with one of Georgia's most beloved and quirky annual celebrations: the Putnam County Dairy Festival. Equal parts agricultural heritage, community celebration, and small-town fun, the Dairy Festival is the event that defines Putnam County's identity as proudly, distinctively itself.

Why a Dairy Festival?

Dairy farming has been central to Putnam County's agricultural economy throughout the 20th and 21st centuries. The annual festival was established to celebrate that heritage, honor local dairy farmers, and bring the community together around shared traditions. It's now nationally recognized as one of Georgia's distinctive small-town festivals.

What to Expect

The festival typically features live music on multiple stages, a carnival midway with rides and games, food vendors serving everything from classic festival fare to Georgia-specific treats, arts and crafts vendors, a dairy-themed parade, and — of course — plenty of dairy products to sample. The crowning of the Dairy Festival Queen is a beloved annual ceremony.

When and Where

The Dairy Festival takes place annually in Eatonton, typically in late spring. Check the official Putnam County tourism website or the festival's social media pages for the exact dates each year, as they can vary. The event is centered in downtown Eatonton around the courthouse square, with activities spreading throughout the surrounding area.

A Local Perspective

If you've just moved to Putnam County — or if you're visiting for the first time — the Dairy Festival is the fastest way to understand what this community is about. It's unpretentious, genuinely fun, and full of the kind of people who've been coming to this same event for decades. Don't miss it.
